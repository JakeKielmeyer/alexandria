Section 01
Platform Overview
A browser-based creation and hosting tool for adult visual storytelling. Creators build stories in an editor, stories live at hosted URLs, and creators share those links directly with their audience. There is no public discovery feed.
Positioning
Adult-focused in the HBO sense — horror, grimdark, dark romance, explicit sexual content, gore, psychological thrillers, mature themes. Content that is banned or restricted on every mainstream platform. This is the core positioning and the reason the target creator audience has nowhere else to go.
V2 Scope
The first build is webtoon format only. Comic and book formats are planned for V3+ but are explicitly out of scope for this design phase.

Cover screen
Cover art: Full-screen. Supports static image, GIF, and looping video. Video covers: autoplay, loop, no controls, no sound. object-fit cover — no letterboxing.
Enter interaction: Click or tap upward arrow only. No auto-advance. No timeout. Arrow points up — implies scroll-down reading direction naturally.
No links: No links of any kind on the cover screen. Creator links belong on the end page only.
Audio: Creator can upload background atmospheric audio for the cover screen. Respects the Video & SFX Audio toggle set in the interstitial.
Transitions: In — fade through Void as interstitial fades out. Out — cover art recedes with rotateX tilt, first panel rises from below (same 3D mechanic as chunk transitions).
Webtoon reader
Panel layout: Always full viewport width in V2. Heights are creator-defined (presets + custom). Stack vertically, no gaps unless creator adds spacing. Scroll direction: down.
Video panel behavior: Autoplay at 50% viewport visibility. Pause and reset on scroll out. Only most-visible panel plays when multiple are in view. Always autoplays regardless of sound state — interstitial interaction satisfies browser gesture requirement.
Chunk navigation: Creator-defined breaks. Optional chapter title per break. Two advance methods: scroll past last panel (auto-advance) or next arrow in navbar. Progress stored in localStorage.
Audio classification: Ambient/atmospheric sound → Video & SFX toggle. Composed music → Music toggle. Both default Off. Reader opts in deliberately.

Section 09
Editor Architecture Locked April 17, 2026
Pivot — April 17, 2026
The editor has been significantly simplified for MVP. Text overlays, speech bubbles, thought bubbles, title cards, narration overlays, and the font editor have all been removed. The editor now focuses exclusively on media composition: uploading image, GIF, video, and audio into pre-sized panel frames, with multiple media items per panel managed as layers.
What the editor does — MVP scope
Creators build panels by uploading media (image, GIF, video, audio) into pre-sized panel frames. No text tools. No speech bubbles. No font editor.
Multiple media items per panel — a panel can contain a background image, a foreground video with transparency (PNG workaround for MVP), an audio track, etc. Each media item is a layer.
Layers — each media item on a panel has a z-order position. Creators manage z-order in the Layers tab of the right rail. Layers can be reordered and deleted.
Drag handles — media items can be repositioned and resized on the canvas using drag handles. Two modes: Fill (fills the panel frame) or Custom (free position and size via drag).
Audio — attached to a panel. Not visible to the reader. Toggled on/off via the speaker icon in the reader navbar.
Transparent video — deferred post-MVP. PNG with transparency covers the foreground layering use case for now. Logged as known limitation.

Reader audio — single source of truthOne store flag: readerStore.videoSfxEnabled. Both the Interstitial "Video & SFX" switch and the Navbar speaker button write to it. Legacy audioEnabled/toggleAudio removed.
Muted computation in readereffectiveMuted = panel.muted || !videoSfxEnabled. Creator-set muted=true is a hard-override the viewer cannot undo. Viewer's videoSfxEnabled only unmutes layers that the creator left muted=false.
Video muted syncImperatively set videoRef.current.muted = effectiveMuted in a useEffect. React's muted prop is an initial-attribute-only in some browsers — updating the prop after mount does not always reach the element.
Autosave layers payloaduseAutoSave.ts layer UPDATE must include autoplay, loop, muted, playback_rate. Omitting any of them silently drops the editor's corresponding toggle. Rediscovered as the root cause of the "audio isn't working" bug.
Default muted for new video layersfalse. New videos defer to the viewer's Video & SFX preference (off by default in the reader). A creator who needs a silent clip — e.g. ambient loop under a music track — flips the Muted switch in EditorRail to ON. Image/GIF defaults left at true (no audio track); Audio layer default false.
Autoplay policy compatibilityBecause videoSfxEnabled defaults to false in the reader, first-render effectiveMuted = true even for a muted=false layer — so <video autoplay muted> succeeds under Chrome's policy. User gesture (Navbar speaker click or Interstitial accept) unmutes while the video is already playing, which the policy permits.
Exhaustive-deps on stable settersPreferred scoped eslint-disable-next-line react-hooks/exhaustive-deps with a one-line justification over widening the dep array with Zustand setters that are stable across renders. Applied in Editor.tsx, Reader.tsx, useReaderData.ts.
